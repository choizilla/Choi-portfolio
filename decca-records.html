Intro

Established in 1929 by Edward Lewis, and now part of Universal Music Group, Decca Records is home label both historic and modern classical artists.

Popular artists include Jazz signers Gregory Porter, Ella Fitzgerald and Jeff Goldblum, pianist Ludovico Einaudi, "King of the Waltz" violinist and conductor Andre Rieu, Italian opera tenors Luciano Pavarotti and Andrea Bocelli, as well as contemporary artists including Aurora, Ólafur Arnalds and much much more.

Brief

Looking to break into the e-Commerce business as a label and serve their current fans a broader music discovery experience, the label requested for a department style store which houses all three of their major brands—contemporary music label Decca Records, classical label Decca Classic, as well as post-classical label Mercury KX. The store would also house smaller upcoming artists alongside Decca's well known repertoire in order to cross promote them to fans of similar genres.
