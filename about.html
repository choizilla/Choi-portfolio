How I work

From the experience of working in both tech and creative teams, in order to truly optimise efficiency between the different roles in a product building team, I found that designers such as myself should not heavily rely on UI design softwares such as Invision, Adobe XP, or Sketch. Learning the fundamentals of programming and basic ideas of how code and systems work allows designers to understand technical restrictions, which allows us to find areas to creatively exploit.

By harnassing both my creative and technical sides, I can now take an idea from whiteboard straight to build—with a little help from team mates and minimal use of graphic software (Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator) inbetween. I've developed a set of unusual skills to fast-track communication and project development, creating beautiful visuals with amazing user experience in mind.

I’m a great addition to teams with designers who can’t code and developers who struggle with visual design. I can help simplify technical jargon for the wider team too.
