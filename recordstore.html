Record Store

Redesigning an online vinyl record shop founded in 1998.

www.recordstore.co.uk

About

Record Store boasts to be "the best record emporium on the good ol' web". They emerged as an oasis for music fans unable to find their latest fix on the highstreet, specialising in the best new bands and the cream of established alternative artists, becoming one of the leading online music retailers in the UK. Their demographic lean towards artists in the unconventional and underground genres, include Cigarettes After Sex, The Specials, Fontaines D.C., JOHN, Murder Capital, Thom Yorke and Neneh Cherry.

Did you know? The vinyl record market is propped up by “superfans” which accounts for 2/3 of all vinyl sales.

Brief

As the online store has not been loved or up-kept for over 5 years, some areas of the design and features were beginning to look and feel outdated. Usability wise, it serves it purpose in selling music, and excels in terms of product range. On top of that, they are excellent at content curatation for emails.

There are two parts to the project, refreshing the brand to create a cohesive system with character which works across all media types, as well as updating website to compete with other online physical music sellers. Another consideration is the shift to mobile visitors and consumers

The difficulty is in finding the balance of tweaking and improving weak areas, versus a complete overhual. For this instance, we took an iterative approach, aiming to build a solid foundation first before considering nice-to-haves.

  • Refresh the Record Store brand
  • Revamp the webstore
  • Improve current mobile experience on store side

Analysing the orignal

Previous home page

Overall, the old home page layout served their audience well and so it makes sense to maintain some continuality to avoid needlessly distrupting the current user flow. On the other hand, we need to focus on improvementing the navigation which appears across the online store.

We could look at optimising page load speeds which has been affected by the image heavy design.

Previous Product page

The product page was also one of our focus points when it came to the redesign to drastically improve content hierachy and clarity of text and imagery.

Previous Product list

The original layout already follows the conventional product list layout in rows and colomns of 3-4 on desktop, so the issue for these pages lie with the inconsistent use of product images. With a multitude of background colours, perspectives, duplication scattered across the page, we needed a solution to unify the images. The result should aim to provide better visual cues for users to quickly indentify the format of the item, for example; cassette tape, vinyl, double LP or CD.

Solution

Updating Product visuals

One of the most important aspects of visual design is the photography/imagery used within a layout to sell the product. As humans primarily use sight and visuals to cue their actions, we need to put more emphasis on images which had issues of duplication, inconsitent perspectives and sizing.

My first course of action was to setup a library of easy to use templates for the store managers so that their products can start looking more uniform. To ensure they understood how the templates worked, I then trained them step by step and gave them guidance when they were unsure.

Changes include:

  • Transparent background PNGs so we can control the colour via code instantly.
  • Consistent lighting and perspectives.
  • Contextual sizing, ie CDs are smaller than vinyl records.
  • Visually anchoring the products on a baseline.
  • Transparent vinyl sitting above the sleeve to show that it's see-through.

Navigation improvements

Years worth of data indicated a large percentile of visitors would interact with the search feature on the home page to look for artist name, release name, as well as empty searches to display all products available on the shop.

In the new design, we wanted to bring the search to the users attention by increasing the size drastically which also leads to easier access due to it's size. We also included smart suggestions based on what has been typed.
